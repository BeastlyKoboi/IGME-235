About Me
King of having ideas,
but forgetting to write them down!

Lovely to meet you! My name is Allan Flores and I am a 2nd year Game Design and Development major at RIT, with a planned minor in Web Development. I am endlessly fascinated with the storytelling abilities of games and the underlying systems that make them a reality. I hope to contribute meaningfully to the space, as well as doing it within a team of talented individuals. I have experience with C#, Java, Unity, Visual Studio, and will be studying more C++ soon. I am very technically minded and meticulous, but I intend to persist outside my comfort zone into refining my sense of design and creativity. With every project I take on, I learn greatly from the struggles involved and the incredible people that surround me. It is my lifelong goal to find a way to give people a sense of fulfillment and ease in their life through my creations, a goal that I am well on my way to achieving.

Projects

Mine to Five!

A drilling game based off of Drillionaire, made in Unity as a personal project. You play a 9-5 worker who must drill to find ore to meet resource quotas and to sell for upgrades. All assets are custom made. There is a proof of concept WebGL build available and a viable version is in the works.

Quotes of Note!

A web-based word game based off of a mobile game called Figgerits, made as a personal project. There are currently ten levels of historical quotes and with more topics to come like movie and book quotes.

A Fine Match

A card-matching battler created with PixiJS as an academic project. You play a young cardcaster that is making their way through their first battles. Match cards to cast spells, which have all sorts of effects. Effects like extending the time before the next attack, attacking, healing hearts, and adding dodge chance. All assets were custom made specifically for this game.

Earth Keeper

An asteroids-esque shooter game created in unity and with an existing WebGl build. You are controlling the Hestia, a spaceship that must protect the earth from asteroids. You must shoot rockets to destroy them, and make sure to avoid hitting earth yourself. All assets are custom made and this project is still currently in production. New mechanics and features coming soon.

Greedy Gamer Gizmo

A tool created using the Cheapshark API to help frugal gamers find the best discounts and deals on the games they want. This website searches a variety of stores with many different searchable parameters, including max price, minimum rating, name, release date, savings, and more! All thanks to Cheapshark for making it possible.

Spellblade

A side-scrolling 2D platformer I worked on as a member of a team of four in Monogame. My primary areas of responsibility were the level rendering, file management, and later the animations. You would slash, blast, and jump your way through each level, until facing the unique bosses. As you traverse each level, you can improve an ability to make the future easier.

The Fountain of Objects

A topview exploration game in which the player must find and restore the fountain of objects, then return safely to the beginning. An early personal challenge that drew much inspiration from a practice exercise in the C# Players's Guide by RB Whitaker. In the textbook, it was a text-based game based on one the first computer games called "Hunt the Wumpus." I decided to make a graphic version to make it more fun for me, and to test myself.

Minesweeper

This was a personal project I decided to try out over the summer, to keep my knowledge of Monogame fresh. It was a clear imitation of the popular game, but made with custom assets. I specifically chose this project because I wanted to attempt to make something complete without assistance. The need for recursion when uncovering blocks of tiles was an obstacle I was proud to get past alone.
